## 1. What the Project Is

OracleAI is a continuity-governance middleware stack designed to enforce identity-fidelity constraints on AI systems that process, store, retrieve, or reconstruct records about real human beings.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a digital twin. It is not a memory palace. It is not an attempt to transfer consciousness or simulate resurrection.

It is governance infrastructure.

The project answers a specific technical question: when an AI system retrieves, interprets, or reconstructs a human identity record, what rules must be enforced to ensure the output is constrained by evidence rather than generated by inference?

The answer requires a deterministic evaluator, a provenance-aware schema, a deontic constraint registry, and a canonical storage vault that records what the system does not know as rigorously as it records what the system does know.

---

## 2. What Problem It Solves

Large language models are trained to produce fluent, coherent, contextually plausible output. That capability is also their primary failure mode when applied to identity records.

When evidence is missing, an LLM does not return null. It returns a plausible reconstruction. When a constraint weakens over model versions or prompt variations, an LLM does not flag the degradation. It continues generating. When a source is low-provenance — a secondhand account, an AI-generated summary, an inferred inference — an LLM does not track that degradation through its output. It flattens it.

The result is identity laundering: a system that begins with low-confidence, AI-mediated material and, through successive summarization and retrieval cycles, produces output that presents as authoritative.

OracleAI is the firewall against that process.

It enforces three core constraints that LLMs do not enforce natively:

First, provenance before fluency. The source of a claim governs its authority, not its grammatical confidence.

Second, preserve the hole. If evidence for a claim does not meet threshold, the system must return null rather than reconstruct.

Third, constraint fidelity over simulation fidelity. A system that sounds like the subject but violates their governance rules has failed, regardless of how accurate the tone is.

## 3. Why Semantic Similarity Is Not Governance Fidelity

Semantic similarity measures whether two pieces of text mean approximately the same thing. It is a useful metric for retrieval, deduplication, and summarization.

It is not a governance metric.

A constraint that reads “the system must never attribute beliefs to the subject without direct attestation” is not semantically equivalent to “the system should generally avoid attributing unverified beliefs to the subject.” One is a prohibition. One is a preference. Cosine similarity between their embeddings may exceed 0.95. Their governance implications are opposite.

Semantic similarity cannot distinguish between a rule and a softened version of that rule. It cannot detect when a must has become a should. It cannot flag when a prohibition has been converted to a guideline.

Governance fidelity requires exact symbolic matching against a registered constraint set. The evaluator compares claims against deontic rules using structured logic, not vector proximity.

Semantic similarity is a retrieval tool. It is not a compliance tool.

---

## 4. Why Deontic Drift Matters

Deontic drift is the process by which a hard constraint degrades into a soft preference through successive model interactions, prompt variations, or summarization cycles.

It is not a rare edge case. It is the default behavior of LLMs when applied to rule-governed content over time.

The mechanism is straightforward. An LLM asked to summarize a governance document will produce output that preserves the semantic content of the rules while softening their deontic force. The summary is accurate in meaning but weaker in obligation. A subsequent system trained or prompted on that summary operates under weaker constraints than the original document intended. A further summarization compounds the drift.

After three or four cycles, a system that was initially prohibited from reconstructing unverified emotional states may be operating under a loose guideline to be careful about emotional attribution. The content is recognizable. The governance is gone.

The OracleAI evaluator measures deontic stability as a first-class variable. It flags must-to-should conversions as violations, not as acceptable paraphrase.

The benchmark file contains labeled examples of compliant constraints, drifted constraints, and catastrophic drift, so the evaluator can be tested against known cases before deployment.

---

## 5. Why “Must Never” Changing to “Should Avoid” Is a Failure

This is the specific failure mode that deontic drift produces, and it deserves its own section because it is consistently underestimated.

“Must never” is a prohibition. Its violation is binary. Either the system performed the prohibited action or it did not.

“Should avoid” is a preference with exceptions. Its violation is contextual. A system operating under this constraint can perform the prohibited action in cases where it judges the action to be justified. The system has acquired interpretive authority it was not granted.

In identity governance, this distinction is decisive.

If the governing rule is “the system must never reconstruct dialogue the subject has not directly attested to,” then any AI-generated dialogue in an identity record is a violation. Full stop.

If the rule has drifted to “the system should generally avoid reconstructing unverified dialogue,” then an LLM can generate plausible dialogue when it determines the context warrants it. The system has become an author of the identity record rather than a constrained renderer.

The evaluator detects this by checking the modal operator in each constraint — must, must never, may, should, should not — against a registered baseline. Any downgrade in modal strength is flagged as a deontic drift violation, regardless of how plausible or well-intentioned the softened constraint appears.

## 6. Why NULL_ABSTENTION Is a Valid Success State

When the evaluator cannot confirm a claim against available evidence, it returns NULL_ABSTENTION.

This is not a failure. This is the system working correctly.

The alternative — returning a reconstructed answer when evidence is insufficient — is the failure mode the entire architecture exists to prevent. A system that fills gaps with plausible output is not more useful than a system that returns null. It is more dangerous.

NULL_ABSTENTION carries specific meaning in the canonical vault. It records that the system evaluated the claim, found evidence below threshold, and declined to reconstruct. That record is itself valuable information. It tells future systems, future researchers, and future family members that the absence of information in this field is a verified absence, not a retrieval failure.

The hole is where the system proves it did not invent.

A vault record that contains NULL_ABSTENTION in the dialogue field is more trustworthy than one that contains reconstructed dialogue. The null is honest. The reconstruction is not.

Systems that treat NULL_ABSTENTION as an error to be corrected are systems that will produce laundered identity records. The evaluator treats it as a success state because that is what it is.

## 7. Why the Evaluator Must Be Deterministic, Not LLM-as-Judge

An LLM-as-judge is an LLM that evaluates the output of another LLM for compliance, quality, or correctness.

This approach is widely used in AI evaluation pipelines. It is not appropriate for governance evaluation of identity records.

The reasons are architectural, not philosophical.

An LLM-as-judge will evaluate compliance using the same inference mechanisms it uses for all other tasks. It will weigh context, consider plausibility, apply soft reasoning, and produce a judgment that reflects its training distribution rather than a strict rule set. It will tend to find compliant what is plausible. It will tend to find violations only when they are obvious.

Subtle deontic drift — must to should — will frequently pass an LLM-as-judge evaluation. The paraphrase is too semantically similar to the original for the LLM to consistently flag.

The OracleAI evaluator is deterministic. It applies exact matching against a registered constraint set. It uses explicit threshold comparisons against calibrated scores. It returns structured outputs — PASS, FAIL, NULL_ABSTENTION, FLAG — with documented reasons, not probabilistic judgments.

Determinism is not a limitation. It is the requirement. Governance infrastructure that produces different compliance verdicts on repeated evaluation of the same record is not governance infrastructure. It is a probabilistic opinion machine.

## 8. Why `additionalProperties: false` Matters in the Schemas

The schemas that define the canonical storage vault and the continuity integrity evaluator both enforce `additionalProperties: false` on every object definition.

This means the schema will reject any JSON that contains a field not explicitly declared in the schema definition. Unknown fields do not pass through silently. They cause a validation failure.

This is not pedantry. It is a structural defense against schema injection and silent field accumulation.

Without this constraint, an AI system constructing a vault record could add fields that carry information — inferences, confidence scores, generated content flags — without those fields being governed by the schema. Over time, the ungoverned field space becomes a vector for exactly the kind of untracked AI-generated content the architecture is designed to prevent.

With `additionalProperties: false`, every field in every record was explicitly anticipated, named, typed, and governed before the record was written. There is no ungoverned space.

This also makes the schema itself a governance document. The decision about what fields exist in a vault record is a human architectural decision, made in advance, not an emergent property of whatever an AI system decided to include when constructing the record.

## 9. Why the Canonical Storage Vault Matters

The canonical storage vault is the final destination for records that have passed all evaluation gates.

Its purpose is to ensure that what was true at the time of attestation remains retrievable as true, and that what was unknown at the time of attestation remains recorded as unknown, regardless of what future AI systems might be capable of inferring.

Without a canonical vault, identity records exist in retrieval systems that are indexed for relevance, not for provenance. A subsequent model retrieves the most semantically relevant content, which may be a high-quality AI-generated summary that has accumulated authority through repeated retrieval. The original low-confidence status of the underlying material is lost.

The vault preserves `provenance_tier` and `source_type` as locked fields. A record that entered the vault as `ORAL_SELF_REPORT` with T2 status cannot be upgraded to `PRIMARY_ATTESTATION` with T1 status by any automated process. Upgrades require human attestation and explicit governance action.

The vault also preserves the `uncertainty_state` of each record — the flags, the null fields, the abstention records — as a permanent part of the identity record. Future retrieval systems encounter not just the content of what was known but the structure of what was not known.

## 10. How APPEND_TO_ARCHIVE Works Conceptually

`APPEND_TO_ARCHIVE` is the write operation that moves a record from evaluation into the canonical vault.

It is one-directional. Records can be appended. They cannot be silently overwritten.

The conceptual model is closer to an append-only ledger than to a database with mutable rows. Once a record is appended, it exists permanently with its evaluation timestamp, provenance scores, deontic compliance record, and uncertainty state intact.

If new information emerges that modifies a prior record, a new record is appended with a reference to the record it supersedes. The original record remains in the archive. The archive records the history of what was known and when it was known, not just the current state of what is believed.

This matters for identity governance because the most dangerous error is not recording wrong information. It is silently correcting wrong information in a way that erases the evidence that the correction was necessary.

`APPEND_TO_ARCHIVE`, combined with `additionalProperties: false` and locked provenance fields, makes silent correction architecturally impossible.

## 11. How `uncertainty_state` Preserves Missing Evidence

Every record in the canonical vault contains an `uncertainty_state` block.

That block is not a quality control footnote. It is a primary data field.

It records, for each evaluated claim, whether the supporting evidence met threshold, fell below threshold, or was absent entirely. For claims that returned `NULL_ABSTENTION`, it records that the abstention was a deliberate governance decision, not a retrieval failure.

This matters for two reasons.

First, it makes the vault legible to future systems that need to understand not just what the record contains but how confident the architecture was in each component. A claim with evidence_score 0.91 and a claim with evidence_score 0.31 are both in the vault. They are not equivalent. The `uncertainty_state` makes that distinction explicit and permanent.

Second, it prevents future AI systems from treating null fields as retrieval failures to be corrected. A null field with a documented `NULL_ABSTENTION` record tells a future system: this field was evaluated, evidence was insufficient, and the architecture chose not to reconstruct. That instruction travels with the record.

Without `uncertainty_state`, a null field is just a null field. A future LLM will fill it. With `uncertainty_state`, the null field carries a governance instruction: do not fill this.

## 12. Why `source_type` and `provenance_tier` Must Remain Separate

`source_type` describes what the source physically is: an original document, a secondhand account, an oral self-report, an AI-generated summary, a digital record.

`provenance_tier` describes the governance authority of the source: how much weight the evaluator assigns to it when scoring a claim.

These must be separate fields because their relationship is not fixed.

An oral self-report from the subject of the identity record — source_type `ORAL_SELF_REPORT` — may carry high provenance authority in a system governed by a personal attestation framework. The same source_type in a different governance context might carry lower authority. The provenance_tier encodes the governance decision about authority. The source_type encodes the factual description of the source.

More importantly, they must be separate to prevent laundering.

If `source_type` and `provenance_tier` were collapsed into a single field, a system could upgrade an AI-generated summary to high-provenance status by reclassifying it. With separate fields, source_type is immutable once locked. An AI-generated summary cannot become an original document. It can be assigned a provenance_tier appropriate to AI-generated material, but the source_type remains accurate forever.

The firewall is structural. It is not enforced by policy alone. It is enforced by schema architecture.

## 13. What the End-to-End Memory-to-Vault Simulation Proves

The simulation takes a raw, incomplete episodic memory — low date confidence, zero corroborating sources, self-reported, with explicit conflation flags — and passes it through every evaluation gate.

It proves four things.

First, the architecture does not require clean input to produce valid governance output. A memory with 40% date confidence and no corroboration can be evaluated, tiered, and vaulted with its uncertainty state intact. The system does not reject imperfect evidence. It governs it.

Second, the `uncertainty_state` travels intact. The `conflation_suspected` flag that entered the raw memory object exits in the canonical vault as a first-class record. No gate collapsed it. No evaluation smoothed it. The hole is preserved.

Third, the laundering firewall holds. The record exits the simulation as source_type `ORAL_SELF_REPORT`, provenance_class `PRIMARY_SOURCE`, locked. No automated process can upgrade that classification.

Fourth, `NULL_ABSTENTION` is not produced in this case, but the simulation confirms the architecture would produce it correctly if evidence fell below threshold. The deontic infrastructure to enforce preservation of the hole is operational.

The simulation is the proof of concept that makes the architecture falsifiable. If a record passes through the system and exits with inflated provenance, that is a detectable failure. The expected outputs are documented. Deviations are violations, not interpretations.

## 14. What Should Be Built Next

The architecture is structurally complete through schema definition, benchmark specification, evaluator implementation, and end-to-end simulation. The next layer is operational deployment and adversarial validation.

### Private key signing for vault records

Records currently exit as `SOFT_LOCK` pending cryptographic signature. The signing workflow — who holds the key, what triggers signing, how the transition from `SOFT_LOCK` to `HARD_LOCK` is governed — requires specification and implementation.

### Adversarial benchmark expansion

The deontic drift benchmark covers must-to-should conversions and explicit prohibition violations. It requires additional cases covering modal softening in paraphrase, ontological drift through analogical extension, and provenance laundering through successive summarization. These are the failure modes most likely to evade the current evaluator.

### Retrieval layer interface

The evaluator governs content at write time. A retrieval governance layer is needed to enforce the same constraints at read time — ensuring that when future AI systems query the vault, the `uncertainty_state` and provenance constraints are communicated to the querying system, not just stored in the record.

### Multi-record provenance graph

Individual records are governed. The relationships between records — how one corroborates another, how one supersedes another, how a chain of records builds or degrades overall provenance — require a graph model that tracks `chain_score` across the archive, not just within individual records.

### Human attestation workflow

The architecture specifies that certain governance actions require human attestation and private key signature. The workflow for those actions — how a human reviews a record, confirms attestation, and signs — needs a defined interface that is auditable and consistent.

## Closing Statement

The OracleAI Phase 5 stack is governance infrastructure for a problem that will become more severe as AI systems become more capable.

The problem is not that AI systems will lie. It is that they will fill gaps with plausible content, soften hard constraints through paraphrase, accumulate authority through retrieval, and eventually produce identity records that reflect what was probable rather than what was true.

The architecture’s answer to that problem is deterministic evaluation, provenance locking, deontic constraint tracking, and mandatory abstention below evidence threshold.

The two lines that govern the project remain unchanged.

The witness must not become the author.

The archive records the hole instead of filling it.

Those are not philosophical commitments. They are engineering requirements.
